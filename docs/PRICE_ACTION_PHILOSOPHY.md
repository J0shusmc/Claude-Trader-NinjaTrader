# Price Action Trading Philosophy - Gap Fill Theory

## Core Concept: Markets are Gap-Filling Machines

The fundamental principle underlying this trading system is that **price is always heading toward filling a gap**. Markets are efficient, and inefficiencies (gaps) create imbalances that price must eventually resolve.

## The Two Types of Magnets

### 1. Fair Value Gaps (FVGs)
**What is a Fair Value Gap?**
- An inefficient price zone where no trading occurred
- Created when three consecutive candles show a gap
- Represents unfilled orders and price imbalance
- Acts as a MAGNET that draws price back

**Why Price Fills Gaps:**
- Market seeks equilibrium
- Unfilled orders remain in the gap
- Algorithms target these inefficiencies
- Historical support/resistance is created

### 2. Key Psychological Levels
**Round Numbers as Magnets:**
- 14500, 14600, 14700, 14800, etc.
- These are "whole levels" that traders watch
- Act as natural support/resistance
- Price often pauses or reverses here

**Why Key Levels Matter:**
- Human psychology (round numbers are easy to remember)
- Large orders cluster at these levels
- Algorithms target these zones
- Historical significance builds over time

## The Trading Thesis

### Gap-Fill Mentality
Always ask yourself:
1. **Where is the nearest unfilled gap?**
2. **Is price heading toward it?**
3. **Are there key levels in the way?**

### The Perfect Setup: Confluence
The BEST trades occur when:
- **FVG Zone** aligns with **Key Level**
- Example: Bearish FVG at 14600 (support)
- Example: Bullish FVG at 14700 (resistance)

This creates **double confluence** - two magnets at the same price!

## Risk Management with Gap Theory

### Position Sizing Based on Gap Quality

**High Confidence (2 contracts):**
- FVG + Key Level confluence
- Fresh gap (< 50 bars old)
- Clear path to target gap/level
- Trend alignment

**Medium Confidence (1 contract):**
- FVG only (no key level)
- Older gap (50-100 bars)
- Some obstacles to target
- Neutral trend

**Low Confidence (0 contracts - NO TRADE):**
- No clear gaps nearby
- Price between multiple gaps
- Gap already partially filled
- Conflicting signals

## Advanced Concepts

### Gap Hierarchy
Not all gaps are equal:

1. **4-Hour FVG** > 1-Hour FVG (bigger timeframe = stronger)
2. **Gap at Key Level** > Gap away from key level
3. **Fresh Gap** (< 25 bars) > Old Gap (> 75 bars)
4. **Large Gap** (> 5pts) > Small Gap (< 3pts)

### Partial Gap Fills
Sometimes price only fills part of a gap:
- Watch for rejection at 50% of gap
- Full fill is more common but not guaranteed
- If partial fill holds, gap remains active

### Gap Clusters
Multiple gaps in same zone = SUPER MAGNET
- Price will return with high probability
- Use widest gap range for entries
- Strongest support/resistance

## The Edge

Why this works:
1. **Market Efficiency**: Gaps must be filled for balance
2. **Order Flow**: Unfilled orders remain in gaps
3. **Psychology**: Key levels attract human traders
4. **Algorithms**: Programs target these inefficiencies
5. **Self-Fulfilling**: As more traders use this, it becomes stronger

## Trading Checklist

Before every trade, verify:
- [ ] Clear unfilled gap identified
- [ ] Price heading toward gap (not away)
- [ ] Gap age < 100 bars (still active)
- [ ] Key level proximity noted
- [ ] Risk/reward > 2:1
- [ ] Stop beyond gap zone
- [ ] Target = next gap or key level
- [ ] Position size appropriate for confidence
- [ ] No conflicting gaps in the way

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Trading without gap thesis** - always know which gap price wants
2. **Ignoring key levels** - they matter as much as gaps
3. **Chasing filled gaps** - entry is already gone
4. **Oversized positions** - not all setups are equal quality
5. **No target plan** - know your exit before entering

## Summary

**The Core Truth**: Price moves from gap to gap, level to level.

**The Strategy**: Wait for price to approach an unfilled gap (especially near a key level), enter when it arrives, target the next gap/level.

**The Edge**: Markets are predictable in their quest for efficiency. Gaps create inefficiency. Price seeks to restore balance.

This isn't speculation - it's trading the mathematics of market structure.
